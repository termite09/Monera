# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Privacy-minded Revolut-primary spenders in the UK/EU, paid on a fixed payday. Tech-literate enough to export a bank statement. They want to understand their spending — not just see their transactions — but refuse to link their bank to another fintech or trust a third party with their financial data. They've likely tried a spreadsheet and abandoned it.

Primary job: after payday, understand where the money went and whether they stayed in budget. Between paydays: check "safe to spend" and catch silent subscriptions.

Anti-persona: non-Revolut users, multi-bank power users who need real-time sync, people who want a fully hands-off auto-linked app.

## Product Purpose

Monera turns a Revolut CSV or Excel export into a clear, payday-aware picture of where your money goes — budgets, category breakdowns, merchant analytics, subscription detection, and plain-language insights — with every byte stored in a private folder in the user's own Google Drive. There is no Monera backend, no database, and no account data on any third-party server.

Success: a user imports their first statement and immediately understands their spending in a way they couldn't from their bank app alone. Business goal: grow adoption among Revolut/privacy-minded users while free; monetize later via a hosted version and optional premium tier.

## Brand Personality

Calm, precise, trustworthy. Privacy-confident without being preachy. Honest and meticulous — respectful of the user's intelligence. Like a brilliant friend who happens to know finance: gives you real numbers and real explanations, not motivational fluff or vague reassurances.

## Anti-references

- **YNAB / spreadsheet-y:** Utilitarian, US-centric, functional but not inviting — the design communicates "tool" before "product." No spreadsheet energy.
- **Mint / classic budgeting SaaS:** Bright greens, busy infographics, dated mid-2010s fintech energy.
- **Preachiness:** Designs that lecture users about saving money or celebrate "streaks" and "scores" are off-brand. Monera surfaces data; it doesn't judge.
- **Badge-heavy privacy marketing:** "Your data is safe!!" shield-icon-on-every-surface layouts imply distrust. Privacy is architectural — explain the mechanism in plain copy, don't badge-stamp it.

## Design Principles

1. **Trust is felt, not announced.** Clean, precise, unhurried layouts communicate reliability before a word is read. Let the architecture speak; don't layer security claims on every surface.
2. **Data clarity over decoration.** Every visual element exists to make financial numbers easier to understand. Decoration that doesn't serve comprehension is removed.
3. **Friendly precision.** Warm, human micro-typography and copy — never approximate or vague, always concrete. Explain the number behind the figure.
4. **Payday-native, not calendar-native.** The UI maps to how people actually think about money: pay periods, not months. Language and hierarchy reinforce this.
5. **Invite without pushing.** The landing page earns trust through candour — what it is, what it isn't, open-source — rather than pressure. Confidence, not urgency.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Keyboard navigable throughout. Visible focus states on all interactive elements. Reduced-motion alternative for all animations. Body text contrast ≥4.5:1; large text ≥3:1. Financial data conveys meaning via shape, label, and value — not colour alone — so it works for common colour-vision deficiencies.
